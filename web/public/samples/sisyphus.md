# The Stone Remembers — excerpt

At dawn I put my hands where they had always gone, into the two shallow places the mountain had taught the stone to keep for me. The granite was cold. It had been dreaming of snow.

I pushed, and somewhere beneath the weight, a memory opened: not mine, but the stone's. It remembered every hand that had cursed it, loved it, abandoned it halfway up the slope. It remembered the gods before they learned to call themselves eternal.
